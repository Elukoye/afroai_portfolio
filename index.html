Hurricane Prediction using Deep Learning

Predictive Analytics

Overview

Hurricanes cause massive destruction and loss of life, by studying previous weather data, one can predict the expected number of hurricanes in a season. These predictions help authorities prepare for disasters and over the years, minimums the loss of lives and properties. Weather data is extremely complex,chaotic and has thousands of parameters.
Machine learning model based on deep neural networks is used to predict the number of hurricane occurrences in a season. More than 100years of climate data was used for training the model and tested it on 5 years data. Results of an accuracy of 79% was achieved.

Objective

This project was aimed at predicting the number of hurricanes in a hurricane season by using machine learning algorithms.

Method and Result

For prediction, data from 1901 to 2010 that contained 6 weather variables is used. These variables are U wind speed, V wind speed, 2-metre pressure, sea surface temperature, mean sea level pressure and sea ice cover. These are the main determinants of the nature and intensity of hurricanes. The labels for the regression models are derived from the total number of storms for each month from the extracted dataset.

The CNN model used is designed with 6 convolutional layers and 4 fully connected layers. The Deep Neural Network was trained to classify the hurricane season based on the frequency of occurrence. Below is a visualization (with 60% occurrence) of predictions made by the model for 5-year period.

This prediction had an accuracy of 79%.

Detection of Stationary Aircraft using Deep Learning

Oject Detection

Overview

Machine Learning has become a key component for Spatial analysis in GIS. Detection and recognition of regions and objects (natural and manmade) from satellite images has find many applications in different fields. For military applications, detection of stationary aircraft in airports can be essential. Using deep learning an algorithm was developed to detected aircrafts from satellite images collected from airports in Europe.

Objective

This project was aimed at developing an algorithm that can detect stationary aircraft using deep learning.

Method and Results

Pre-Processing

For the training process, 100 aircraft (known class feature) were extracted and labelled using an open-source annotation tool, LabelMe. Since the satellite images acquired were large and hence making it difficult for training on the network, the images were divided into chips of size 150 x 150 pixels before feeding into the network. The centroid of the bounding boxes was clustered using DB-Scan algorithm because of the small number of the boxes in the images.

Information about the bounding box is stored in a .txt file in YOLOv3. The object number in every label files (mask) was set to 0 because one category was to be detected (aircraft).

Training

The model is trained on the Darknet Framework, which is an open sources neural network framework. The model was trained on a 2080 Ti GPU, for 28000 iterations with an average loss around 0.9-1.0.

Results

An accuracy of 83% was obtained locating the aircraft on an airport.

Conclusion

Machine Learning, computer vision and satellite imagery combined, can be designed and trained for new solutions and to increase awareness.

Location Analysis of Potential High Altitude wind Power

Time Series Analysis

Overview

For the past decades, ground-based conventional wind turbine systems have been used in wind power generation. Although they face several limitation e.g. visual and noise impacts, space requirement, intermittency, location restrictions linked to environmental and permit issues, and lastly shadows. With improved technology and increased funding, research into wind harnessing is now being directed into high altitude winds to minimize the limitations.

GIS (Geoinformation Systems) enables the advanced processing, analysis and mapping of potential areas to deploy Airborne wind energy systems (infrastructure)- AWESs. The analysis was carried out with datasets extracted from 10 years (2007-2016) with a 6-hour spatial interval and altitudes between 200m-1000m with 100m interval.

Objective

The main objectives of this project were to identify favourable areas for the deployment of Airborne wind energy systems; and to examine the optimal altitude range for the implementation of AWESs.

Method and Results

The data obtained was of GRID format, which is converted into NPZ format for easy usage. For vertical dimensions, the already reanalysed dataset was in atmospheric pressure, which by using the hydrostatic and gas law equations was converted to meters.

The variables in the extracted gridded wind data include atmospheric pressure, time, geopotential, temperature, wind speed and direction. Using the wind speed, mass, area and volume wind power density is computed. Time series analysis is performed to analyse trend, seasonality and irregularity. Statistical analysis is performed to determine a). regression and correlations between wind speed and wind power, and height and with power, b). Weibull probability function done to ascertain the best approximate for the distribution of wind speeds over time c). other statistics include mean and standard deviation.

With constraints imposed by the government e.g. distance from transport infrastructure, electricity grid, settlement and urban area, and airports, military training areas and controlled airspace etc .

Buffering is done to these areas and the locations that are constraints.The power output highly depends on the strength of wind speed, with the decrease in wind speed (Southern Germany), so the decrease of wind power output. The wind speed increases from the south and so is the expected wind power. At this level, its both economical and viable to choose Northern Germany as the best position to implement AWESs.

Results

Wind power generated from 600m to 1000m agl (above ground level) shows a relatively constant and high AWE Production.
